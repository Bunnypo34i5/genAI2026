## 1. Fire Safety Command — Building Improvement Divisions

**Teams:**
- Fire Safety Command (Tel: 2312 0376)
- Building Improvement Division 1 (Tel: 2716 2970)
- Building Improvement Division 2 (Tel: 2104 2755)
- Building Improvement Strategy and Special Operations Division (Tel: 2312 0376)

**Relevance:** These teams are responsible for building fire safety compliance and improvement programmes under the Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance. Wang Fuk Court falls under their purview as a pre-1987 building subject to mandatory inspection.

### Questions:

1. **Building Inspection Records:** How many buildings have been inspected under the Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance in each of the last 3 years (2023, 2024, 2025)? What percentage of inspected buildings were found non-compliant?

2. **Wang Fuk Court Specific:** Was Wang Fuk Court inspected under the Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance prior to the November 2025 fire? If so, what were the findings and what follow-up actions were taken?

3. **Renovation Phase Safety:** What protocols exist to ensure fire safety compliance in residential buildings undergoing major renovation works? Specifically, how are fire service installations monitored during renovation?

4. **Non-Compliant Buildings:** What follow-up actions were taken for buildings found non-compliant in the last 3 years? How many improvement notices were issued and how many were enforced?

5. **Building Improvement Strategy:** Following the Wang Fuk Court fire, has the Building Improvement Strategy and Special Operations Division revised its approach to older buildings undergoing mandatory renovation?

## 2. Public Safety and Communication Division

**Teams:**
- Public Safety and Communication Division (Tel: 2197 3699)
- Information Unit (Tel: 2724 1466)

**Relevance:** This division handles public safety messaging and communication strategy, including fire safety education campaigns and public engagement.

### Questions:

6. **Fire Safety Education Coverage:** How many fire safety education sessions or community talks were conducted in 2024-2025? How many residents were reached? Please provide breakdown by district.

7. **Older Building Outreach:** Are fire safety education programmes specifically targeted at residents of older buildings (pre-1987)? If so, how many such sessions were held in 2024-2025?

8. **Wang Fuk Court Education:** Were any fire safety education sessions, fire drills, or community training conducted at Wang Fuk Court in the 3 years prior to the fire (2022-2025)?

9. **Elderly Residents:** What programmes specifically target elderly residents? Given that nearly 40% of Wang Fuk Court residents were 65+, how does FSD ensure fire safety messages reach this demographic?

10. **Budget Allocation:** What is the annual budget allocation for fire safety education and community engagement versus emergency response operations?

11. **Effectiveness Measurement:** How does FSD measure the effectiveness of its fire safety education programmes? What metrics are used? Are there any outcome evaluations?

## 3. Licensing & Certification Command

**Teams:**
- Licensing & Certification Command (Tel: 2733 7619)
- Fire Protection Engineering Compliance Division (Tel: 2367 3206)
- Fire Protection Facilities Supervision Division (Tel: 2722 1915, 2312 0219)
- Policy Division (Tel: 2723 2197)
- Prosecution Section (Tel: 2369 9402)

**Relevance:** These teams oversee fire service installation contractors, certify fire protection facilities, and prosecute non-compliance. Critical for understanding how fire alarms at Wang Fuk Court were deactivated.

### Questions:

12. **Fire Alarm Certification:** How does FSD verify that fire alarm systems are properly certified and maintained in residential buildings? How often are inspections conducted?

13. **Deactivation Protocols:** What are the protocols for temporarily deactivating fire alarm systems during building renovation? Who must be notified and what safeguards must be in place?

14. **False Reporting:** In the last 3 years, how many cases of false reporting by fire service installation contractors have been identified? What penalties were imposed?

15. **FS251 Certificates:** How does FSD verify the accuracy of FS251 certificates (annual fire service installation inspection certificates)? Are spot checks conducted?

16. **Contractor Oversight:** How does FSD assess the competence and track record of registered fire service installation contractors? Is there a public register of contractors with compliance records?

17. **Prosecution Statistics:** How many prosecutions have been brought under the Fire Services Ordinance for non-compliance with fire service installation requirements in 2023-2025?

## 4. Outsourcing and Complaints Section

**Team:**
- Outsourcing and Complaints Section (Tel: 2367 0973, Email: seo_oc@hkfsd.gov.hk)

**Relevance:** This section handles public complaints about fire safety. Residents at Wang Fuk Court raised concerns for over a year before the fire.

### Questions:

18. **Complaint Records:** How many complaints related to fire safety in residential buildings undergoing renovation were received in 2024-2025?

19. **Wang Fuk Court Complaints:** Did FSD receive any complaints from Wang Fuk Court residents about fire safety concerns, fire alarm deactivation, or flammable materials before the November 2025 fire?

20. **Complaint Follow-up:** What is the standard procedure and timeline for investigating and responding to fire safety complaints from residents?

21. **Escalation Protocols:** When a complaint involves potential life safety risks (e.g., deactivated fire alarms in occupied buildings), what escalation procedures exist?

## 5. Mobilising and Communications Division

**Teams:**
- Mobilising and Communications Division (Tel: 2733 7726)
- Fire Services Communications Centre (Tel: 2311 0066)

**Relevance:** These teams manage emergency communications and could be involved in decisions about activating alert systems during fires.

### Questions:

22. **Emergency Alert System:** Does FSD have the authority to request activation of Hong Kong's Emergency Alert System (EAS) during a fire emergency? If yes, under what circumstances?

23. **EAS During Wang Fuk Court:** Was activation of the EAS considered during the Wang Fuk Court fire? If not, why not?

24. **Communication to Residents:** During a Level 5 fire, what mechanisms exist to alert residents in surrounding buildings (beyond the building fire alarm systems)?

25. **Digital Alert Capabilities:** Is FSD exploring any digital alert technologies (e.g., mobile push notifications, location-based alerts) as a supplement to traditional building fire alarms?
