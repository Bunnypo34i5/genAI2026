Framing Our Report as a LegCo Complaint

GCAP 3056 — Spring 2026 · Week 5

The Big Picture

Begin with the end in mind: Our final report will be framed as a complaint to the LegCo Public Complaints Office. We are arguing that the government could have done a better job — supported by our research evidence.

What is the LegCo Redress System?

  • The Legislative Council operates a Redress System to receive complaints from the public who are aggrieved by government actions or policies
  • It also handles representations on government policies, legislation, and other matters of public concern
  • Duty Members (groups of nine, rotating weekly) oversee the system and meet with complainants
  • All case information is kept strictly confidential

Our Report Structure

The report should follow this general structure (replace functional headings with thematic headings for your topic):

1.
Title — thematic, reflecting the core complaint
2.
Executive Summary — the problem, the gap, what should change
3.
Background: The Problem — publicly available evidence on the issue
4.
Existing Solution — what the government is currently doing
5.
Room for Improvement — hypotheses supported by evidence
6.
Government's Replies to Our Enquiries — analysis of responses (or non-responses) under Code on Access to Information
7.
Complaints and Follow-up — what lawmakers should push for
8.
Suggestions for Media Investigation and Coverage — how to create pressure for accountability

What We Can Complain About

  • Government actions or decisions that affect stakeholders negatively
  • Programmes not meeting their stated objectives
  • Government staff who have not responded adequately to public concerns
  • Gaps between policy goals and actual implementation

What is Outside Scope

  • Requests for legal advice or legal services
  • Private disputes or court decisions
  • Matters handled by independent bodies (e.g. ICAC, IPCC)
  • Labour disputes (unless of wide public concern)
  • Matters outside HKSAR jurisdiction

Key Decisions for Each Team

  • What specific decisions and programmes to focus on? — narrow from a broad topic to specific government actions
  • In what ways can the government do a better job? — your hypotheses, supported by public info and research
  • What questions to send to government? — under the Code on Access to Information (aim for replies before the end of the semester)
  • How to frame the complaint? — thematic headings that tell a story, not just generic labels
